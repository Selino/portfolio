My Bookshelf

Refactoring: Improving the Design of Existing Code

It's nice to have a basis for making refactoring decisions beyond "it just feels right". Martin Fowler has compiled a great stack of proven design patterns and the logical reasons to employ them in an easy to follow format. I find his writing easy to digest as well.

Siddhartha

I read and re-read this story continuously. Learning to carry the many lessons between these pages through personal and professional life has become a daily goal.

The Five Dysfunctions of a Team

An ex-coworker turned me onto this. I've found that one of the best ways to avoid pitfalls is to study the success and failures of others. I've found this book useful for not only managing my place on a team but also evaluating teams BEFORE I join them. Good stuff.
